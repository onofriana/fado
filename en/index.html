Name

I am
Onofriana

I was born from a name that History almost let fall into silence. Maria Severa Onofriana was the first voice that Fado recognised as its own. Severa, the name the myth glorified. Onofriana, quieter, more intimate, recalls part of a story that remains to be told.

That is my name for the world.

I want to bring Fado to places where it can once again be heard and appreciated — not as background, distraction or curiosity, but as living cultural presence, with pulse and memory.

Fado is not staged.
It happens.

Tradition has no meaning without purpose, and our commitment is to integrity, intention and professionalism.

We work with those who want to offer something with weight — not a performance, a moment with memory.

Fado

Heritage
with responsibility

Fado has been Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity since 2011. Not by decree — by recognition. Because there was something there worth preserving: a way of being with music found nowhere else.

That recognition carries responsibility.

Fado is not scenery. Not background to a conversation that continues. When Fado enters a room, the room changes. Or it should.

That change is what Onofriana seeks — in every place it performs. Not Fado as product. Fado as event.

Tradition is not preserved in museums. It lives in the moments when someone treats it with the seriousness it deserves.

1820 — 1846

Maria Severa Onofriana
the first fadista

✦

Maria Severa Onofriana was born in 1820. Daughter of a fishwife, she grew up between the street and music. She sang in the tascas of Mouraria and in the salons of the Count of Vimioso. She died in 1846, at twenty-six, in Rua do Capelão.

History kept the name Severa. The myth set about glorifying it — novels, theatre pieces, the first Portuguese sound film. Severa became figure, symbol, narrative.

Onofriana remained in shadow. Less conspicuous, less malleable for myth. It is the name of a person — not a character.

That is why it is ours.

We did not choose the name history celebrated. We chose the one it almost lost. There is a position in that: Fado does not begin in myth. It begins in the voice. And the voice had a full name.

Presence

Who we
work with

We work with companies, municipalities, festivals and cultural institutions. The starting point is always the same: a conversation about the place, the moment and who will be listening.

The musicians Onofriana chooses are not chosen by chance. There is a criterion — silent, exacting — that determines who enters this territory. We do not seek availability. We seek what is rare.

Marta Rosa

© Robnu Creative Studio

The artistic direction of Onofriana is the responsibility of Marta Rosa — fadista, viola de fado player, lyricist and composer. Graduated in Musical Sciences from Universidade Nova de Lisboa and in Singing from Escola Superior de Música do IPL, she built her career across fado houses, composition and artistic direction. One of the few women to play viola de fado professionally.

She was responsible for the curation and programming of Povo Lisboa, one of Lisbon's reference fado houses. She was a member of the group Fado As Mariquinhas and has presented Onofriana at municipalities and corporate events throughout Portugal.
