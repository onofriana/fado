I was born of a name that History almost let fall into silence.

Maria Severa Onofriana was the first voice that Fado claimed as its own. Severa — the name myth glorified. Onofriana — quieter, more intimate — carries a part of that story still waiting to be told.

That is my name for the world.

I want to go where Fado is truly heard and felt — not as background noise, distraction or curiosity, but as a living cultural presence, with pulse and memory.

About Us

Onofriana

Onofriana is dedicated to the curation, production and programming of Fado events in cultural, institutional and private contexts. Its name is inspired by the mythical and enigmatic figure of Maria Severa Onofriana, the first fadista documented in the history of Fado.

We work with an extensive network of experienced fadistas and instrumentalists to form ensembles tailored to each context: intimate acoustic sessions, stage performances, traditional and thematic fado evenings, shows for schools, theatres and auditoria, and local festivities.

We set ourselves apart through artistic quality and distinctive programming — from all-female Fado ensembles to the exclusive creation of personalised fado lyrics to honour people or mark special occasions.

Artistic Direction

Marta Rosa

Onofriana's artistic direction is the responsibility of Marta Rosa — fadista, viola player, lyricist and composer. A graduate in Musical Sciences from Universidade Nova de Lisboa and in Voice from the Escola Superior de Música of the Instituto Politécnico de Lisboa, she has built her career across fado houses and other stages, in creation and artistic direction. She is one of the very few women to play the fado viola professionally.

She was responsible for the curation and programming of Povo Lisboa, one of the city's landmark cultural venues and home to numerous artistic residencies that shaped the paths of several leading fado voices, including her own. She was a founding member of As Mariquinhas, an exclusively female fado group, and now presents Onofriana to the world — the feminine legacy of Fado.
