About

I am
Onofriana

Onofriana is dedicated to the curation, production and programming of Fado events in cultural, institutional and private contexts. The name is inspired by the mythical and mysterious figure of Maria Severa Onofriana, the first documented Fado singer in the history of Fado.

We work with an extensive network of experienced fadistas and instrumentalists, forming different ensembles adapted to each context: intimate acoustic sessions, stages, traditional and themed Fado nights, performances for schools, theatres and auditoriums, local festivities, and more.

We set ourselves apart through the quality and range of our programming — from all-female Fado ensembles to the exclusive creation of personalised fado lyrics celebrating people or special occasions.

I was born from a name that History almost let slip into silence.
Maria Severa Onofriana was the first voice Fado recognised as its own.
Severa, the name the myth glorified. Onofriana, more discreet, intimate, recalls a story that remains to be told.
That is my name for the world.

I want to go wherever Fado can be listened to and appreciated — not as background, distraction or curiosity, but as a living cultural presence, with pulse and memory.

Fado is not performed.
It happens.

Tradition has no meaning without purpose.

Marta Rosa · Onofriana

Artistic Direction

Marta
Rosa

The artistic direction of Onofriana is the responsibility of Marta Rosa, fadista, viola player, lyricist and composer. Holding degrees in Music Sciences from Universidade Nova de Lisboa and in Singing from the Escola Superior de Música at the Instituto Politécnico de Lisboa, she built her career between fado houses and other stages, composition and artistic direction.

She is one of the very few women to play the viola de fado professionally.

She was responsible for the curation and programming of Povo Lisboa restaurant, one of the city's most celebrated cultural spaces and home to multiple artistic residencies that shaped the paths of several fado voices, including her own. She was a founding member of Fado As Mariquinhas, an all-female group, and now presents Onofriana to the world — the feminine heritage of Fado.
